#### [Submission URL](https://cells2pixels.github.io/) | 198 points | by [esychology](https://news.ycombinator.com/user?id=esychology) | [51 comments](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48567877)

Neural Cellular Automata: From Cells to Pixels (EPFL + Google Research, SIGGRAPH 2026)

What’s new
- The authors pair a classic Neural Cellular Automaton (NCA) running on a coarse grid with a tiny implicit decoder (a Local Pattern Producing Network, LPPN) that turns local cell states + local coordinates into per-pixel attributes (e.g., color, normals). Result: the same NCA can render at arbitrary resolution, in real time.

Why it matters
- Traditional NCAs shine at self-organization (growth, regeneration) but hit a wall at high resolutions: memory/training scale quadratically with grid size, information propagates only locally, and real-time inference is expensive.
- Decoupling “dynamics” (coarse NCA) from “rendering” (lightweight local decoder) keeps the system fully local and massively parallel while breaking the resolution barrier. You get high-res textures and patterns without simulating millions of cells.

How it works
- Evolve a small set of per-cell latent states on a coarse lattice (2D grid or mesh vertices).
- For any output pixel/point, interpolate neighboring cell states and feed them, plus the point’s local coordinates, into a shared MLP (the LPPN).
- Train NCA and LPPN jointly end-to-end with task-specific losses:
  - Morphogenesis: grow from a seed toward a target, preserving NCA’s hallmark regeneration/robustness.
  - Texture synthesis: produce photorealistic, tileable, and view-dependent attributes (including PBR channels) with minimal memory overhead.
- Because both steps are local, inference scales well and remains highly parallelizable on GPU.

What you can do with it
- Real-time, high-resolution procedural textures driven by NCA dynamics.
- Interactive “painting” via local edits/brushes that the system regenerates around.
- MeshNCA: render textures directly on 3D meshes; also supports 3D grids for volumetric textures.

Notable points
- Preserves the self-organizing behavior NCAs are known for while making outputs arbitrarily high-res.
- Works across 2D images, 3D grids, and meshes; demos include PBR textures, growth from seeds, and 3D textures.
- Team includes Alexander Mordvintsev (known for earlier NCA work). Paper on arXiv; code and interactive demos available.
