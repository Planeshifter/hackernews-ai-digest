### I prompted ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, and Gemini and watched my Nginx logs

#### [Submission URL](https://surfacedby.com/blog/nginx-logs-ai-traffic-vs-referral-traffic) | 134 points | by [startages](https://news.ycombinator.com/user?id=startages) | [22 comments](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47835646)

Do chatbots actually visit your site when asked about it, or just answer from their own index? To find out, the author wired up an nginx “probe” with a custom log format and queried major assistants with unique URLs. They tracked two distinct signals: provider-side fetches (the bot hitting your origin) vs. human clickthroughs (a user following a cited link).

What happened, by assistant:
- ChatGPT: Announces as ChatGPT-User/1.0 and bursts requests from multiple Azure IP ranges, often fetching several candidate pages before citing. Single-IP rate limits will miss this.
- Claude: Announces as Claude-User/1.0, always checks /robots.txt first, follows redirects cleanly, and honors disallow. Distinct from Claude-SearchBot and ClaudeBot; only Claude-User indicates a real user query.
- Perplexity: Perplexity-User/1.0 fetched directly (no Accept, no referrer). PerplexityBot separately checked robots.txt. Perplexity can also answer purely from its index.
- Meta AI: meta-webindexer/1.1 fetched without a robots.txt precheck in this test.
- Manus: Manus-User/1.0 appended to a Chrome-like UA; fetched without a robots precheck.
- Gemini: No provider fetches observed; answers came from Google’s existing search index. Google has no retrieval-specific UA; any live fetch would look like Googlebot. Blocking Google-Extended doesn’t block Googlebot.
- Copilot: Fetched as a plain Chrome browser with full asset requests; no distinct Copilot UA and no Bingbot during the window.
- Grok: Fetched as plain Mac Safari/Chrome; indistinguishable from a human visit.

Takeaways for site owners:
