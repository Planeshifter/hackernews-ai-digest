DiffusionGemma: experimental text-diffusion model promises 4x faster local generation

- What’s new: The Gemma team released DiffusionGemma, an Apache-2.0, 26B MoE model that generates text via diffusion-style parallel decoding instead of left-to-right tokens. It drafts 256 tokens at once and iteratively refines them with bi-directional attention and self-correction.

- Speed headline: Up to 4x faster text output on dedicated GPUs by shifting the decode bottleneck from memory bandwidth to compute.
  - Reported throughput: 1000+ tokens/sec on a single NVIDIA H100; 700+ tokens/sec on a GeForce RTX 5090.

- Hardware footprint: Although the total model is 26B parameters, only ~3.8B are active per step. With quantization, it fits in ~18GB VRAM, making high-speed local inference feasible on high-end consumer GPUs.

- Where it shines: Low- to medium-batch, local, interactive workflows—inline editing, rapid iteration, code infilling, non-linear structures (e.g., amino acid sequences, graphs). The bi-directional attention makes tasks like Sudoku easier than for autoregressive models; the team highlights a fine-tuned Sudoku solver.

- Trade-offs: Overall output quality is lower than standard autoregressive Gemma 4. Best for speed-critical use; use Gemma 4 for max quality or fine-tune DiffusionGemma for specific tasks. Parallel decoding offers diminishing returns in high-QPS cloud serving.
