In a thought-provoking paper titled "ELIZA Reinterpreted," Jeff Shrager challenges the common assumption that ELIZA was the world's first chatbot. He argues that Joseph Weizenbaum, ELIZA's creator, did not intend to invent a chatbot but rather a platform for studying human-machine conversation and cognitive processes.

Shrager provides a historical context for ELIZA's development, highlighting its origins in AI history. He discusses how ELIZA's accidental release into the world, coupled with misconceptions about its purpose, led to it being labeled as a chatbot for over 50 years.

This reevaluation of ELIZA's origins sheds new light on its significance in the history of AI and prompts a reexamination of the narratives surrounding early chatbot technology.
