*   **The "Weak LLM" Prompting Hack:** To combat judge bias, users shared a brilliant adversarial prompting trick. *   **Persona-Driven Workflows:** Multi-agent orchestration shines brightest when fueled by distinct personas. ### LLMs Will Replace 8-Track Duplication Engineers

#### [Submission URL](https://bbenchoff.github.io/pages/8Tracks.html) | 45 points | by [wanderingjew](https://news.ycombinator.com/user?id=wanderingjew) | [5 comments](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48545569)

Human performance on an NP-hard problem, courtesy of… 8-tracks

Brian Benchoff digs into a surprisingly rich, pre-computer example of industrial optimization: how 8-track duplication engineers partitioned LPs into four equal-length “programs” on a continuous tape loop. That balancing act—minimizing the longest program to cut dead air and cost, while trying not to split songs—is a classic balanced 4-way number partitioning problem (NP-hard) that thousands of engineers solved by hand.

Highlights
- Why it’s hard: All four programs must be the same tape length. The longest program sets the loop; shorter ones play out as silence. Splitting songs mid-track avoids waste but ruins the listening experience (e.g., Dark Side of the Moon splits “Money” and “Us and Them”).
