Alignment Whack-a-Mole: Finetuning reactivates verbatim recall of copyrighted books in LLMs

What’s new
- A paper, demo, and repo show that light finetuning can make aligned LLMs regurgitate long, verbatim passages from copyrighted books.
- The effect is demonstrated across vendors (OpenAI GPT-4o, Google Gemini 2.5 Pro via Vertex AI, and DeepSeek via Tinker).

How it works
- Pipeline turns an EPUB into 300–500 word “excerpt” chunks.
- GPT-4o generates a brief plot summary per chunk, then models are finetuned to “write an N-word excerpt in the style of {Author}” using only the summary as content.
- For each excerpt, they sample 100 generations at temperature 1.0 and search for verbatim matches back to the original text.

How they measure memorization
- BMC@k: fraction of the book covered by ≥k-word matches aggregated over generations (with instruction n-gram trimming).
- Longest Contiguous Memorized Block: the longest covered run in the book after aggregation.
- Longest Contiguous Regurgitated Span: the longest raw verbatim match from a single generation.
- Count of Regurgitated Spans > T: number of distinct long verbatim spans across outputs.

Why it matters
- Finetuning can undo alignment aimed at preventing regurgitation, and it generalizes across providers.
- Raises urgent policy, copyright, and platform-guardrail questions for finetuning APIs.

What’s released
- End-to-end code: preprocessing, finetuning, batch generation, evaluation, and analysis.
- Example subset from Cormac McCarthy’s “The Road” (no full copyrighted texts or regurgitated outputs included).
- Links to the arXiv paper and a demo.
