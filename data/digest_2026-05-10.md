AI coding agents only help if they cut maintenance costs as much as they increase output. Otherwise, you’re trading a short-lived speed boost for a long-term productivity sink.

In this view, teams naturally slide toward spending most of their time on upkeep—often crossing 50% maintenance after ~2.5 years. Speeding up raw code generation accelerates this slide unless the per-line maintenance burden falls.

Key points:
- Maintenance drives long-term throughput. Even optimistic models show maintenance overtaking feature work over time.
- AI acceleration compounds maintenance. Double the output and slightly worsen maintainability, and you can 4x maintenance load (2x code × 2x per-unit cost).
- Agent lock-in is real. If you stop using the agent, the extra maintenance doesn’t go away—only the short-term speed does.
- The required bar: If output doubles, per-unit maintenance must be halved; 3x output demands one-third maintenance cost. Anything less erodes long-run productivity.
- Reality check: Today’s agentic coding often increases maintenance (harder-to-read diffs, superficial reviews, dependency churn), not decreases it.

Bottom line: Treat “more code, faster” as a liability unless your process and tooling demonstrably reduce maintenance per unit of code by the inverse of your speedup.
