- What he found: PyCharm’s “Full Line Completion” plugin (local model) repeatedly suggests insecure urllib3 patterns. - The core question: If an AI code assistant proposes insecure code, is that a vulnerability deserving a CVE? Larson argues CVEs aren’t the right tool—but treating this as “not security” risks deprioritizing a real safety problem.

- Bigger picture: He suspects similar issues exist across code-gen tools. The trust users place in IDE suggestions means insecure completions can easily slip into production.

- Implication for vendors: Don’t rely on downstream linting alone—mitigate at the source. That could mean blocking known-dangerous completions (e.g., disabling cert checks), context-aware safety filters, or surfacing stronger guardrails and warnings.

- Takeaway for developers: Treat AI suggestions as untrusted input. Keep security linters on, resist disabling verification, and scrutinize convenience completions—especially around networking, crypto, and auth.
